Privacy Principles

The policy on this page applies to the United Kingdom, and your use of Stella will follow these principles. If this is not your correct country, use the sidebar to switch your region and language.

Introduction

This privacy policy aims to help you understand how Stella manages and handles your data, information and usage of Stella Services.

Who's who?

"We"/"Us" refers to Ceccun Limited in the United Kingdom.
"You" refers to the individual user accessing and sending data into our services either manually or automatically.
"Services" refers to the user-accessible products and platforms created or hosted by Stella.

Welcome

We are committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This privacy policy covers the experience we provide for users age 13 and over on our services.

What information do we collect?

We automatically collect information when you first visit or use a service. We also collect information you submit to us manually.

Information we automatically collect.

We only collect information automatically while you are using a platform to technically tailor sites and data to be optimised for the device that is receiving information from us. Defined below:

  • Device Information - We collect information about your device including your IP address, screen dimensions, device browser, operating systems, time zones, language settings and geolocation related data.
  • Location data - We collect information about your location, including location information based on your IP address. With your permission, we may also collect Global Positioning System (GPS) data.
  • Cookies and local storage - We use cookies and your browser's local storage to keep you signed in, remember your preferences, sync your Tabs, and record which cookie/consent choices you have made. Non-essential cookies (such as those that detect your region for local information) are only set after you opt in through the cookie banner. We do not place advertising or third-party tracking cookies on our services.

Information we collect manually

We collect information manually when you provide it to us through forms or interactions you take on the Stella website.

These interactions are listed below:

  • Your full name - We collect your full name at Account Creation to personalise your account and know who you are.
  • Your email address - We collect your email address at Account Creation to give you an identifier to log in again, send you security emails and notifications.
  • Your search queries - When you use Stella search or Blocks, your query text is processed by Stella's search service and, if Blocks is enabled and you are signed in, forwarded to our LLM aggregator. We do not retain your queries on our servers beyond short-lived operational logs, and we do not use them to train any model. See "Search Privacy" and "Blocks Privacy" below for the full data flow.

Search Privacy

When you submit a search query, your query text and basic request metadata (such as your IP address and browser user agent) are processed by Stella's search service. We then forward the query on your behalf to a set of upstream search engines (which may include but are not limited to Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Brave Search and Wikipedia) to aggregate results.

Those upstream engines receive the query and the connection metadata of the forwarded request from us, and may process it according to their own privacy policies. We do not retain your search queries or your IP address on our servers beyond what is required for short-lived operational logs.

When you switch to the Images tab, the same query is processed by Stella search to return image thumbnails. Clicking a thumbnail opens a local preview built from the same response; no additional request is made to Stella's servers when you pan, zoom, or browse related images.

Blocks Privacy

Stella offers an opt-in service called Blocks, which provides short, AI-generated answers alongside your search results. Blocks is disabled by default and only runs after you accept the in-product consent prompt. You can withdraw consent at any time by clearing your local preferences in your browser; doing so disables Blocks immediately for that device.

Blocks when you are signed in to Stella ID

When you are signed in and Blocks is enabled, Blocks sends the following to a Ceccun-operated backend, which then forwards it to OpenRouter (our LLM aggregator) for processing:

  1. Your search query text.
  2. Short excerpts of the top web results returned by Stella search (typically the title and a 200–250 character snippet for the first few results).
  3. Your Stella ID authentication token, which is used by our backend to verify you are signed in. The token is not forwarded to OpenRouter.

OpenRouter routes the request to one of several upstream model providers (such as Cerebras, Groq, Google, OpenAI, Anthropic or Meta) based on availability and latency. Those providers receive only the query text and result snippets; they do not receive your Stella ID, email, name, IP address, or any other directly identifying information from us. The provider may independently log the request according to its own policy.

The generated answer is streamed back to your device and rendered alongside your search results. A short copy of the generated answer is stored only on your device, under the "Blocks History" entry on the Tabs page; it is not synced to Stella ID and is not retained on our servers.

Blocks when you are not signed in

When you are not signed in, Blocks falls back to a simpler knowledge-base lookup. In this mode, only your query text is sent to our backend, which uses natural-language processing and a Wikipedia lookup to construct a brief answer. No LLM provider is contacted in this mode.

What Blocks does not do

  • Blocks does not retain a conversation history on our servers. Each query is processed in isolation.
  • Blocks does not use your queries or generated answers to train any Ceccun-operated model.
  • Blocks is not shown for queries where Stella search already returns a structured infobox; in that case no query or snippet is sent to OpenRouter at all.

Lawful basis for processing

Under the UK GDPR and the EU GDPR, we only process personal data when we have a lawful basis to do so. The basis depends on the activity:

  • Account creation, sign-in and sync — performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b)), so we can deliver the Stella ID service you signed up for.
  • Non-essential cookies and similar technologies — your consent (Article 6(1)(a)), obtained through the cookie banner. You can change or withdraw your choices at any time.
  • Blocks (LLM-powered answers) — your consent (Article 6(1)(a)), obtained through the in-product Blocks consent prompt. You can withdraw it at any time.
  • Forwarding search queries to upstream engines and short-lived operational logs — our legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) in providing and securing the service. We do not use these for marketing or profiling.
  • Legal obligations — where we are required to retain or disclose data by law (Article 6(1)(c)).

Your rights

Under the UK GDPR and the EU GDPR you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how we use it.
  • Right to rectification — to have inaccurate or incomplete personal data corrected.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — to have your personal data deleted, subject to legal limits.
  • Right to restriction of processing — to have us limit how we use your data in defined circumstances.
  • Right to data portability — to receive a machine-readable copy of the account data you provided to us, and to have it transferred to another controller where technically feasible.
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Rights related to automated decision-making — see the "Automated decision-making" section below.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing that took place before withdrawal.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — with a supervisory authority. In the United Kingdom that is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO, ico.org.uk). EU residents may also contact the supervisory authority in their member state of habitual residence.

How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of the rights above, or for any privacy-related question, write to us at privacy@ceccun.com. We will respond within one month, as required by Article 12(3) of the UK GDPR. For account deletion specifically, you can also use the in-product "Revoke" flow from your Stella ID settings, which permanently removes your account and tokens from our servers within 30 days.

International transfers

Stella user-account and sync data is stored on servers operated by Ceccun Limited within the European Economic Area (Ireland and the Netherlands) and the United Kingdom.

For Blocks — only when you are signed in and have opted in — your query text and short result snippets are transferred to OpenRouter and to the upstream model provider OpenRouter routes the request to. OpenRouter and those providers (which may include Cerebras Systems, Groq, OpenAI, Anthropic, Google and Meta) operate primarily in the United States. We do not transfer your Stella ID, email, name, IP address, or any other directly identifying information to these recipients; only the query text and result snippets needed to generate the answer are sent.

These transfers are made under the UK Addendum to the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (where the recipient is certified under the framework), or a combination of both, depending on the recipient.

Upstream search engines (for example Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo and Wikipedia) may receive your query and the connection metadata of the forwarded request when we send search queries to them on your behalf. They process this information under their own privacy policies.

Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as we need it:

  • Account data (name, email, hashed password, Stella ID): for as long as your account is active, and up to 30 days after you delete or revoke the account, after which it is permanently deleted.
  • Sync data (Tabs, settings): for as long as your account is active and sync is enabled. Removed items are permanently deleted from our servers within 30 days.
  • Last-search sync (only when you are signed in): we store your single most recent search query so you can resume it from another signed-in device. It is overwritten on every new search and is not a search history. You can clear it at any time from the dismiss button on the home page, or by deleting your account.
  • Operational logs (request metadata used for security, abuse prevention and debugging): up to 7 days, then automatically purged.
  • Search queries: not retained on our servers beyond the duration of the request, the last-search sync above (signed-in users only), and any short-lived operational logs above.
  • Blocks LLM exchanges: not retained on our servers. Upstream providers may retain logs under their own retention policies; where OpenRouter and individual providers support opting out of provider-side logging on our account, we have enabled that option.
  • Blocks history shown on the Tabs page: stored only on your device in your browser's local storage, and never sent to our servers. You control its lifetime by clearing your browser storage.

Automated decision-making

Blocks uses a large language model to generate a short answer from your query and the search-result snippets. This is automated processing, but it does not produce legal effects or similarly significant effects concerning you within the meaning of Article 22 of the UK/EU GDPR. You can opt out of Blocks entirely by not enabling it, or by clearing your local Stella preferences after enabling it.

Withdrawing consent

Where we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing that took place before withdrawal:

  • Cookies and similar technologies — open the cookie banner again from the link in the footer (or clear your browser cookies for our domain) and change your preferences.
  • Blocks — clear the "local.settings" entry in your browser's local storage for our domain, or clear all site data for Stella in your browser. Blocks will revert to its disabled state on the next search.
  • Marketing / email communications — we do not currently send marketing emails. If we add them in the future, every email will include a one-click unsubscribe link.

Data breach notification

In the event of a personal-data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of users, we will notify the UK Information Commissioner's Office within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. Where the breach is likely to result in a high risk, we will also notify affected users without undue delay, as required by Articles 33 and 34 of the UK GDPR.

Children

In the European Economic Area the default age is 16, but individual member states may set it between 13 and 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children below the applicable age in their country without a parent or guardian's consent. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data without the necessary consent, please contact us at privacy@ceccun.com and we will take steps to delete it.
